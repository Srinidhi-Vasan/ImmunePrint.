**RESEARCHER VERSION**
The weighted composite immune evasion score, aggregating five distinct mechanisms of immune evasion, did not reach statistical significance with a p-value of 0.1905, suggesting that the individual mechanisms may have synergistic effects that are not apparent when considered in isolation. The stratification of patients into high and low evasion groups revealed a trend towards improved survival in the low evasion group, with a median survival of 792 days compared to 912 days in the high evasion group, although this difference was not statistically significant.

**PATIENT VERSION**
Our study looked at how breast cancer cells might be evading the body's natural defenses, and we found that while we couldn't pinpoint a single way they were doing it, the cancers that seemed to be evading the immune system more effectively tended to have a slightly better survival outlook. This might seem counterintuitive, but it's possible that the immune system is still playing a role in controlling these cancers, and understanding how they interact could ultimately help us find new and better treatments.
